Questions ‚ñº

1. What is the weather like when the hikers start their journey?

Answer: b) Hot

2. What fruit do the hikers hope to find on the tree?

Answer: b) Apple

3. What is under the oak tree?

Answer: c) Branches

4. What do the hikers do to stay dry?

Answer: c) Run under the oak tree

5. What do the hikers make out of branches?

Answer: b) A campfire

6. How do the hikers feel under the oak tree?

Answer: b) Comfortable

7. What do the hikers use to make a bed?

Answer: a) Leaves

8. What happens to the sky during the story?

Answer: a) It turns grey

9. What is the moral of the story?

Answer: b) Sometimes the best gifts aren't what we originally hoped for

10. How do the hikers feel at the end of the day?

Answer: c) Comfortable, warm, and dry

Sentence Building ‚ñº

1. hikers / are / very / very / hungry

Answer: The hikers are very hungry.

2. waiting / they / under / the / oak tree / are

Answer: They are waiting under the oak tree.

3. felt / the / very / tired / hikers

Answer: The hikers felt very tired.

4. see / the / tree / they / in / the / distance

Answer: They see the tree in the distance.

5. made / a / they / campfire / last night

Answer: They made a campfire last night.

6. are / collecting / they / wood / now

Answer: They are collecting wood now.

7. hikers / a / pear tree / thought / the / it / was

Answer: The hikers thought it was a pear tree.

8. the / sky / turning / grey / is / right now

Answer: The sky is turning grey right now.

Script ‚ñº

Narrator:

One hot day, two hikers are walking in the dry, brown hills. They have been walking all day, and they are tired and hungry.

Narrator:

They see a tree in the distance.

Hiker 1:

"I am sooooo hungry! Do you think that's an apple tree? I want a big, crunchy, red apple!"

Hiker 2:

"Nah, that's not an apple tree. It's a pear tree! Pears are my favorite. I want a sweet, juicy, yellow pear!"

Narrator:

But when they arrive at the tree, there are no apples. Or pears either. In fact, there is no fruit at all.

Hiker 1:

"Aw, boo! It's a useless oak tree!"

Narrator:

Just then, the sky turns grey and it starts to rain. The two hikers run under the oak tree to stay dry. It rains and rains, but the oak tree is like a big umbrella. The hikers don't get wet.

Narrator:

They wait under the oak tree for the rain to stop. Day turns into night.

Hiker 1:

"I'm cold. We should make a fire. Help me collect some wood!"

Narrator:

Luckily, there are many old branches under the oak tree. The two hikers collect the branches and make a nice campfire out of them. They sit beside the fire to warm themselves.

Hiker 2:

"I'm tired. We should sleep here tonight. Help me make a bed!"

Narrator:

The hikers make a bed out of soft, green oak leaves. They snuggle into the leaves and fall asleep.

Narrator:

The rain falls and the wind blows, but the hikers are comfortable, warm and dry ‚Äî under the "useless" oak tree.

Moral:

Sometimes the best gifts aren't what we originally hoped for.
